BRIARDS

By Ivy Duckett

The Briand or erger de brie, is a large breed of dog traditionally used as a herder and guardian of sleep.

Breed History

The briard, which is belived to have riginated in France, has been bred for centuries to herd and to protect sheep. The breed was used by the French Army as sentries, messengers and to search for wounded soldiers because of its fine sense of hearing. Briards were used in the First World War almost to thepoint of extinction.

Currently the population of briands is slowly recovering. Charlemagne, Napoleo, Thomas Jeferson and Layfayette owned briards.
